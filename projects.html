academic & professional projects

national collegiate cyber defense competition (nccdc)

participating in the nccdc has allowed me to apply my cybersecurity knowledge in high-pressure environments. by working with my team to defend simulated enterprise networks against real-world cyber attacks, i have gained valuable experience in network security, incident response, and collaborative problem-solving.

collegiate penetration testing competition (cptc)

competing in cptc events at the regional level has enhanced my offensive security skills and broadened my understanding of penetration testing methodologies. reporting and presenting findings to judges has also refined my ability to communicate complex technical concepts effectively.

atlantic council’s cyber 9/12 policy competition

as a two-time semi-finalist in this prestigious competition, i have demonstrated strategic analysis skills in developing policy recommendations for hypothetical cybersecurity crises. this experience has significantly improved my ability to assess risks and devise effective mitigation strategies.

building a cybersecurity lab

i spearheaded a project to build a cybersecurity lab for the cu cyber club using equipment generously donated by the network engineering program. this initiative provided members with a dedicated space to practice hands-on skills and enhanced our club’s ability to prepare for competitions and collaborative learning.

comptia security+ certification

achieving the comptia security+ certification demonstrates my foundational knowledge of cybersecurity principles, including network security, risk management, and incident response. this credential has further validated my expertise and commitment to maintaining industry standards.
